Shelter 1: Leslieville

Leslieville, more “hipster” part, many businesses cater to this, giving more job opportunities to the people who have signed up on the list. Leslieville's atmosphere is laid back with no drug issues or any gang violence. Good area. A lot of semi-detached homes. Can feel homey for the homeless person booking Airbnb.

Shelter 2: Bay Street

Bay Street Corridor, the heart of Toronto’s economy. Close to downtown, lot of job opportunities. In the middle of Toronto, so many things including grocery stores and job postings are within walking distance, not to mention extra circulars and events in walking distance for entertainment. A lot of condos and more construction with new houses always on the market to connect families.

Shelter 3: San Diego

Downtown San Diego provides the most job opportunities and has the highest job density. Has a lot of events since it is the downtown part of the city and includes a lot of grocery stores and useful markets within walking distance. Perfect for someone looking for a job in their area with everything nearby

Shelter 4: London

Liverpool Street. This is the heart of London's stock exchange. There is a lot to explore around the area and a lot of demands for jobs that are easily attainable for homeless people just trying to get back on their feet to start working again. A lot to do and explore along with majorly attainable job opportunities.
